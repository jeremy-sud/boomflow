## ğŸ¯ Automatic vs Manual Badges

### Automatic (verified daily)

These badges are awarded automatically based on GitHub metrics:

- `hello-world`, `first-commit`, `first-pr`, `first-review`
- `week-one`, `month-one`, `year-one`
- `code-ninja` (50 commits), `commit-century` (100), `commit-500`, `commit-thousand`
- `pr-champion` (50 PRs), `review-guru` (100 reviews)
- `kudo-starter`, `kudo-collector`, `kudo-legend`
- `badge-hunter`, `badge-legend`, `badge-master`
- `streak-keeper`, `streak-master`
- `all-star`
- `strong-bond` (3+ peer awards), `team-soul` (10+ peer awards)

### Manual (nomination required)

These badges require nomination by an administrator:

- All **Leadership** badges: tech-lead, architect, visionary, innovator...
- Most **Advanced Coding** badges: algorithm-ace, security-champion, ai-pioneer...
- All **Advanced DevOps** badges: kubernetes-knight, incident-commander, sre-specialist...
- Most **Collaboration** badges: mentor, crisis-averted, hackathon-hero...

### Peer-to-Peer (MANUAL_PEER_AWARD)

These badges are awarded by teammates:

- `resonance`: Awarded directly by a colleague (max 2/year per person)

### Investment (INVESTMENT)

These badges are earned through financial support:

- `patron-seed`, `patron-growth`, `patron-bloom`, `eco-champion`
